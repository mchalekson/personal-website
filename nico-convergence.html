Idea Convergence/Divergence — NICO

I’m exploring how multimodal AI can help teams understand when their ideas converge (align) or diverge (spread out)—and why. The goal is to provide researchers with actionable insight into discussion quality and decision‑making.

Northwestern Institute on Complex Systems (NICO)

Overview

We analyze group discussions captured as text, audio, and video to detect phases of convergence and divergence. We then align human and LLM judgments to quantify agreement and surface concise summaries that explain what moved the team closer together or further apart.

What I built

  • Annotation pipeline for multimodal segments (speaker turns, topics, sentiment, stance).
  • Agreement metrics comparing expert labels with LLM outputs (per‑segment and session‑level).
  • Insight summaries highlighting moments that triggered convergence/divergence.
  • Lightweight dashboards for browsing sessions and exporting figures for reports.

Methods

  • Hybrid labeling: targeted human‑in‑the‑loop checks over LLM first‑pass annotations.
  • Text embeddings + clustering for topic flow; audio features for prosody/turn‑taking dynamics.
  • Simple, transparent agreement stats first (e.g., % match, Cohen’s κ), then task‑specific scores.

Status

We’re iterating on label schemas and evaluation, and packaging the tooling for collaborators at NICO. If you’re interested in collaboration, feel free to email me.
